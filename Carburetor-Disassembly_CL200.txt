DISASSEMBLING CARBURETORS

1. Remove exhaust.

2. Remove side covers.

3. Remove air filters. 

==================================================================================================================================================
Directions that follow apply for either carburetor individually, but note that you MUST KEEP TRACK OF THE LEFT/RIGHT CARBURETOR PARTS SEPARATELY.
SEVERAL PARTS ARE NOT SYMMETRICAL.
==================================================================================================================================================
4. Remove the two screws connecting the carburetor's intake manifold to the engine. These may be JIS screws.

==========================
REMOVAL OF SLIDE ASSEMBLY
==========================
5. Remove the throttle cable from the carburetor by turning the flange counterclockwise and pulling the whole slide assembly from the carburetor. 

6. Disassemble the slide assembly by pulling the spring back, pulling the cable out through the slot, removing the adjuster from the top cap, and removing the cap gasket. 

7. Remove the needle retainer by pushing the slide needle. [~4:00 in the relevant Common-Motor video].

8. Remove the needle. 

===============
CHOKE LINKAGE
===============

9. Remove the cotter pin in the choke linkage. Often these cotter pins are destroyed during removal but these can easily be replaced. Try pushing on one end with a screwdriver and using pliers to pull it out from the other side. 

10. Remove washer.

11. With the cotter pin and washer removed, pull the linkage apart.

12. Remove the fuel/drainage lines. 

13. Remove the intake manifold by removing two screws. These may be JIS screws.

14. Remove the O-ring present on the just-revealed opening in the carburetor that faces the intake manifold. 

15. Remove the idle speed screw. It should have a spring on it; ensure you retrieve the spring.

16. Remove the idle mixture/air bleed screw. Like the idle speed screw, it should also have a spring on it. 

17. Remove the drain plug screw. 

18. Avoid disassembling the part of the choke linkage that attaches to the carburetor. 

===============
FLOAT BOWL
===============

19. Pop the wire clip off of the carburetor.

20. Remove the float bowl of the carburetor, revealing the float.

21. Remove the idle jet ('pilot jet') using a flathead screwdriver.

22. Remove the main jet by placing a 7 mm wrench on the hex nut and using a flat head screwdriver to remove the jet from the emulsifier tube.

23. Remove the emulsifier tube. 

24. Remove the float pin by tapping gently on the side of the pin with a punch, and then pulling it with pliers once it begins to slide freely.

25. Remove the float.

26. Gently remove the float needle with pliers.

27. Remove the needle seat with a socket. (10 mm?)

28. Remove the float bowl gasket.

29. Remove the exhaust nozzle by tapping it gently with a small punch.
