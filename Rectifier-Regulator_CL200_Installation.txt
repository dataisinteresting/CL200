========
This is how I installed the combination rectifier-regulator offered by Common Motor for my CL200, which resolved the inability of my CL200 to recharge the battery.

Product page: https://www.common-motor.com/honda-cb-cl-rectifier-regulator
========

1. Remove seat.

2. Remove battery.

3. Remove exhaust. 

4. Remove side covers.

5. Remove air cleaners (should not need to remove carburetors).

6. Drain tank.

7. Remove tank.

8. Identify regulator. On the CL200, the regulator should be mounted under the battery box. 

9. For the three wires which are connected to the regulator from a wiring harness: pull the three wires from the harness. (a yellow wire, black wire, and green wire).

10. Identify the rectifier. On the CL200, it should be mounted inside the frame under the rear gas tank mount.

11. Disconnect the rectifier's electrical connector (squeeze and pull).

12. Remove the rectifier.

13. Cut the wiring from the base of the rectifier.

14. Strip 1/8-inch of insulation from each wire on the cut end. (2:00 in relevant Common-Motor video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0axV6BiWG0&t=1s).

15. Crimp the included connectors onto the wires using the recommended crimping tool. Watch the 'How to install motorcycle wiring harness electrical connectors' video to ensure you do this correctly. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XJU-LaRYIUI

16. Once the connectors are crimped on, consider applying some new heat shrink tubing.

17. Slide the connectors into the correct spot of the (new?) harness connector. Get it right the first time; difficult to remove them. 

18. Use a round file to enlarge the mounting holes of the new regulator+rectifier.

19. Before mounting the combo regulator+rectifier, remove the black wire from the harness sleeve. 

20. Mount the combo regulator+rectifier to the same location as the original regulator (on the frame below the battery box).

21. Connect the new combo regulator+rectifier to the harness extension and connect it to the old rectifier plug, routing the wiring through the hole in the center of the frame. 

22. Connect the lone black wire to the old regulator's connector for the old black wire. 

23. Cut the green wire from the original regulator.

24. Crimp a standard ring terminal onto the green wire. (4:15 in relevant video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y0axV6BiWG0&t=1s).

26. Plug the green wire into the green female connector to which the old regulator used to be connected. 

27. Attach the ring terminal to the closest bolt on the frame, which is typically one of the the battery box bolts.

28. Save any leftover wiring+bullet connectors for use in later repairs.

29. Reinstall exhaust with new exhaust gaskets.

30. Reinstall tank.

31. Reinstall air cleaners.

32. reinstall side covers.

33. Reinstall battery.

33. Reinstall seat.
